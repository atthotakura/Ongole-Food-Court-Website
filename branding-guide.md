# OFC Branding Guide
## Ongole Food Court — Official Brand Identity Document

---

## 1. Brand Overview

**Brand Name:** OFC  
**Full Name:** Ongole Food Court  
**Location:** Ongole, Andhra Pradesh, India  
**Category:** Quick Service Restaurant (QSR) / Casual Dining  
**Audience:** College students, young adults, families, and working professionals  
**Price Range:** ₹1 – ₹600 per person  

### Brand Personality

OFC is bold, cheerful, and approachable. It is the neighbourhood go-to spot — affordable without feeling cheap, fun without being loud, and familiar enough to visit daily. The brand sits at the intersection of fast food convenience and comfort food warmth.

**Personality Traits:**
- Energetic and vibrant
- Friendly and welcoming
- Casual and unpretentious
- Youthful but family-safe
- Confident and a little playful

---

## 2. Brand Voice & Tone

The OFC voice is conversational, punchy, and fun. It speaks like a friend who knows the best food in town, not a corporate chain.

**Do use:**
- Short, punchy phrases
- Inclusive, warm language ("your treat", "come on in")
- Food-forward excitement
- Colloquial but clean phrasing

**Do NOT use:**
- Stiff, formal corporate language
- Overly technical food descriptions
- Exclusionary language

### Taglines & Slogans

| Usage | Tagline |
|-------|---------|
| Primary slogan | **"Choose your treat before you eat"** |
| Secondary / campaign | **"Don't dream it, eat it in OFC"** |
| Logo sub-text | **"..So Tasty"** |

The primary slogan goes on all printed materials, packaging, and the website header. Secondary slogans are for social media, campaign graphics, and seasonal promotions.

---

## 3. Logo

### Mascot Description

The OFC mascot is a **friendly cartoon chicken** — white-feathered with a red comb, large expressive eyes, an orange beak, and a warm smile. The mascot peeks over or leans on the OFC wordmark ribbon, giving a welcoming, "come eat here!" gesture.

**Mascot personality:** Cheerful, approachable, and a little cheeky. Think of it as the face of the restaurant — always happy to serve.

### Logo Construction

The logo is **circular** and consists of three layered elements:

1. **Outer ring:** Bright yellow/gold circle — creates a bold frame and draws attention
2. **Center banner ribbon:** Deep red ribbon with "OFC" in bold white all-caps lettering, and "..So Tasty" in yellow below it
3. **Mascot chicken:** Positioned above and overlapping the ribbon, as if peeking over it

### Logo Variants

| Variant | Usage |
|---------|-------|
| Full circular logo (mascot + ribbon + ring) | Primary: website header, packaging, signage |
| Wordmark only (OFC text) | Tight spaces: favicons, watermarks, stamps |
| Mascot only | Social media avatars, stickers, campaign art |
| Monochrome (white on red) | Single-colour print, merchandise |

### Clear Space & Minimum Size

- Always maintain a clear space equal to the height of the letter "O" in OFC around all sides of the logo.
- Minimum digital size: **48×48px** for the full circular logo.
- Minimum print size: **2cm diameter** for the full circular logo.

### Logo Don'ts

- Do not stretch or distort the logo
- Do not place the logo on a busy or low-contrast background without a white or red backing circle
- Do not recolour the mascot or change the ribbon colour
- Do not add effects (drop shadows, bevels, glows) to the wordmark
- Do not crop the mascot out of the circular frame in the primary logo

## 4. Colour Palette

The OFC palette is high-energy and appetite-stimulating — reds and yellows are proven to increase hunger and urgency, which suits a QSR perfectly.

### Primary Colours

| Name | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|------|-----|-----|-------|
| **OFC Red** | `#C8102E` | 200, 16, 46 | Primary brand colour — buttons, banners, headers, ribbons, packaging |
| **OFC Yellow** | `#FFD700` | 255, 215, 0 | Accent, logo ring, highlights, call-to-action text on red |
| **Pure White** | `#FFFFFF` | 255, 255, 255 | Text on dark backgrounds, logo wordmark, clean card backgrounds |

### Secondary Colours

| Name | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|------|-----|-----|-------|
| **Deep Crimson** | `#8B0000` | 139, 0, 0 | Gradient partner for OFC Red, section headers on dark backgrounds |
| **OFC Orange** | `#FF6B00` | 255, 107, 0 | Menu boards, section dividers, warm accent panels |
| **Off-White / Cream** | `#FFF8E7` | 255, 248, 231 | Card backgrounds, light mode body areas |

### Neutral Colours

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|-----|-------|
| **Rich Black** | `#1A1A1A` | Body text, fine print |
| **Mid Grey** | `#6B6B6B` | Subtext, captions, secondary information |
| **Light Grey** | `#F4F4F4` | Page backgrounds, dividers |

### Colour Rules

- **Red on White or Yellow on Red** are the primary contrast pairings — always check WCAG AA accessibility (minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio for body text).
- Never use Yellow on White — insufficient contrast.
- Never use Red text on an Orange background.
- Dark Crimson gradient (`#C8102E` → `#8B0000`) is the go-to background for hero sections and full-bleed banners.

## 5. Typography

### Primary Typeface — Display (Headings & Logo-Adjacent Text)

Use a **bold, rounded sans-serif** for headlines. Recommended free alternatives:

- **Nunito ExtraBold / Black** — friendly, rounded, high-energy
- **Fredoka One** — playful and food-brand appropriate
- **Poppins Black** — clean and modern, widely supported

### Secondary Typeface — Body Text

Use a **clean, legible sans-serif** for menu items, descriptions, and body copy:

- **Poppins Regular / Medium** — pairs well with Poppins Black display
- **Inter** — highly legible at small sizes

### Campaign / Slogan Typeface

The slogan "don't dream it, eat it in ofc" uses a **hand-written, informal display font**. For brand consistency, use:

- **Caveat Bold** or **Pacifico** for slogan graphics, chalkboard-style social posts, and campaign art only — not for UI body text.

### Type Scale (Website)

| Element | Size | Weight | Colour |
|---------|------|--------|--------|
| Hero headline | 56–72px | Black (900) | White or Yellow |
| Section heading | 36–48px | ExtraBold (800) | OFC Red or White |
| Sub-heading | 24–30px | Bold (700) | Rich Black or OFC Red |
| Body text | 16–18px | Regular (400) | Rich Black |
| Caption / label | 12–14px | Medium (500) | Mid Grey |
| Button text | 16px | Bold (700) | White (on Red button) |

## 6. Iconography & Graphic Elements

### Ribbon Banner Element

The red ribbon banner from the logo is a reusable graphic element. Use it as:
- Section title backgrounds on the website
- Promotional price badges
- "Special Offer" callout banners

### Circular Frames

Images of food and the mascot work well inside circular or rounded frames, echoing the circular logo shape.

### Dividers & Separators

Use **thin yellow lines** or **short red rules** as section dividers — avoid generic grey lines.

### Background Patterns

For packaging and promotional material backgrounds, consider:
- Subtle geometric patterns in a darker red tone over OFC Red
- Dot/halftone pattern in red/yellow for retro fast-food energy
- Keep website backgrounds mostly clean (white or off-white) so food photography stands out

---

## 7. Photography & Food Imagery Style

- **High brightness, warm tones** — food should look fresh, vibrant, and appetising
- **Close-up shots** of food are preferred (shows texture and detail)
- **Red, yellow, or orange props/backgrounds** for consistency with brand palette
- Avoid cold-toned or heavily desaturated food photography
- Lifestyle photos (people eating, enjoying food) should feel casual and spontaneous, not staged
- Always include the OFC logo or branded packaging in behind-the-scenes and delivery photos

## 9. Menu Board Design

### Colour Zones (Reference: menu assets)

| Zone | Background | Text |
|------|-----------|------|
| Section headers | OFC Red | White |
| Item rows | White / Off-White | Rich Black |
| Accent panel | OFC Orange | White or Black |
| Price badges | OFC Yellow | Rich Black |
| Specials callout | Deep Crimson | White + Yellow accent |

### Menu Layout Principles

- Use a **grid of food photos** with item name, brief description, and price below each
- Combos and special treats get **featured image treatment** — larger photo, coloured background card
- Group by category with a bold red header ribbon between sections
- Prices displayed in bold, right-aligned or badge format

## 10. Digital / Website Guidelines

### Website Colour Application

- **Hero section:** Full-bleed red-to-dark-crimson gradient with white headline and yellow accents
- **Navigation bar:** White background with OFC Red logo and red nav links; on scroll, stick with a subtle red background
- **Body/content sections:** Alternate between white and off-white (#FFF8E7) backgrounds
- **CTA buttons:** OFC Red with white text, hover state shifts to Deep Crimson; secondary CTA in yellow with black text
- **Footer:** Rich Black background with white text and yellow highlight links

### UI Component Styles

| Component | Style |
|-----------|-------|
| Primary button | Red (#C8102E), white text, 8px border-radius |
| Secondary button | Yellow (#FFD700), black text, 8px border-radius |
| Cards | White, 12px border-radius, subtle box shadow |
| Tags / badges | Red or Yellow pill with appropriate text colour |
| Divider | 2px yellow line |
| Input fields | White with 1px mid-grey border, red focus ring |

### Favicon

Use the **circular OFC logo** (mascot + ribbon) at 32×32px and 64×64px. At 16×16px, use the wordmark-only "OFC" on a red circle.

## 12. Brand Don'ts (Quick Reference)

- Don't use fonts that aren't in the approved typeface stack for website/UI
- Don't use OFC Red and OFC Orange side-by-side without a white separator — they clash
- Don't place the mascot in sad, angry, or negative contexts
- Don't use stock photography of generic restaurant settings — OFC imagery should feel local and authentic
- Don't change the slogan wording or punctuation
- Don't produce low-resolution logo exports — always export at 2× or 3× for screen use
